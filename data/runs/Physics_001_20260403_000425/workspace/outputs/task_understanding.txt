Task objective: Reproduce and analyze the core MATBG superfluid-stiffness experiments from the provided simulated dataset, extracting how DC resistance / microwave-derived response and especially superfluid stiffness vary with carrier density (gate tuning), temperature, and applied current, then use those results to assess quantum-geometry enhancement, non-BCS power-law temperature behavior, and current-induced suppression.

Available data in this workspace:
- data/MATBG Superfluid Stiffness Core Dataset.txt: structured simulated arrays grouped into three experiment blocks:
  1) carrier-density dependence with conventional, quantum-geometric, and experimental superfluid stiffness curves for hole/electron doping;
  2) temperature dependence with BCS, nodal, several power-law models, and noisy experimental data;
  3) current dependence with DC and microwave responses compared against Ginzburg-Landau / linear-response models.
- related_work/: four reference PDFs giving context on MATBG superconductivity, unconventional/nodal behavior, topology/quantum geometry contributions to superfluid weight, and device inhomogeneity / twist-angle disorder.

Main planned analysis steps:
1. Parse the text dataset into clean numerical arrays and separate the three experiment sections.
2. Reconstruct the key observables and make overview plots for density, temperature, and current sweeps.
3. Quantify enhancement of experimental stiffness relative to conventional and quantum-geometric expectations.
4. Fit and compare temperature trends against BCS, nodal, and power-law models to identify the best-supported low-temperature behavior.
5. Analyze current dependence to test quadratic suppression / nonlinear response and compare with GL or linear alternatives.
6. Summarize consistency, limitations, and physical interpretation in the context of flat-band superconductivity and quantum geometry.

Expected deliverables:
- Reproducible analysis scripts in code/.
- Intermediate processed outputs in outputs/.
- A report at report/report.md with figures saved in report/images/ covering data overview, main extracted results, and model-comparison / validation plots.
